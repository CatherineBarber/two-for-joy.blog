Finding an identity for this blog (and me)

Rats, cats, magpies Trying to define yourself online is a minefield. Even just picking a name feels impossible. Trying to find something that resonates with you is difficult, trying to find something that resonates but isn’t cringy is very difficult, and anything that fits that brief is guaranteed to be taken already. It’s a very annoying horrible venn diagram. I almost always come back to animals that I identify with. Cats, rats, and birds all appealed to me. Any and all attempts to fit these animals into a name failed miserably (see afformentioned horrible venn diagram). That being said, I feel good with what I’ve settled on. The second line from the magpie nursery rhyme “One for sorrow, Two for joy”. Magpies are definitely in my top 3 birds of all time, and their tendency to pick up shiny things is an accurate parallel to how I pick up new hobbies. I like the idea of a two headed magpie, one head is more reserved, sensible, maybe a bit gloomy. The other head is more eclectic, hopeful, maybe lacking common sense. One head finishes WIPS, the other starts new ones. I’ll try an make a wee logo of them, it’ll be cute. ...

Why start this site

Where else can I go? I’ve always found the wider social media landscape a bit intimidating. I was too young for MSN, never warmed up to facebook, snapchat, insta or whatever came after. I liked youtube, reddit, and discord, where I could enguage in hobby communities without wondering if my family would stumble across a comment I’d written. I never considered myself a heavy social media user. And yet as we come to the halfway point of 2026, the UK faces wide sweeping changes to the social media world. A flat ban for under 16 year olds and, consequently, a requirement to prove that you’re over 16. Given I am not prepared to dish out my government ID to any backwater data broker on the net, I will be using a VPN for as long as that remains an option, and dipping out entirely otherwise. It made me think about how such an important commodity has come to rest in the hands of some of the greediest, most irresponsible people in our society. Big Tech holds the keys to so much of our lives, and our governments have done a historically shit job of protecting us from the greed and irresponsibility of these corporate giants. ...
